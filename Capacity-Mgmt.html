What is Capacity Management?

A mandatory process to ensure we have the right capacity, in the right place, at the right time, for the right cost.

Right Capacity

Sufficient infra to meet performance requirements (NFRs).

Right Place

Correct resource allocation across all architecture layers to avoid bottlenecks.

Right Time

Proactive provisioningâ€”not too early (costs) and not too late (issues).

Right Cost

Avoid significant over-provisioning to manage software, power, and support costs.

Benefits of Effective Capacity Management

Improved Service Availability

Reduce or eliminate capacity-related incidents.

Investment Forecasting

Align investment with budget cycles, avoiding unplanned spending.

Optimized Infra Performance

Identify and resolve bottlenecks by monitoring consumption.

Confident Scaling

Proactively manage risks associated with rapid growth.

Continual Performance Improvement

Drive improvements by highlighting the next constraints.

Our Group Standard Tool: Athene (SCM)

Data Integration

Takes data from various sources for a unified view.

Agile Management

Manages environment at a service-by-server level via dashboards.

Predictive Analytics

Creates forward-looking projections on service performance.

Automated Alerting

"Service View" alerts teams about potential breaches before they occur.

What's Changing? Introducing the CMPF

We are establishing a Capacity Management Practice Framework (CMPF) to ensure consistency and manage services at scale.

Click to enlarge framework diagram

Key Objectives of the New Framework

Business Alignment

Incorporate business growth plans into capacity planning.

Expanded Scope

Move beyond just infrastructure to include IT Asset and Business Service levels.

Consistency

Enable a single, consistent practice across all teams.

Proactive Risk Management

Use KPIs and KCIs to manage risks proactively.

Resource Efficiency

Improve the efficiency of resource allocation.

How It Works

  • The Framework (CMPF) provides the high-level "what" (guidelines, standards).
  • Playbooks provide the department-specific "how-to".
  • Success depends on integration with SDLC, NFRs, and Incident Management.
